# Famous infinite sets

Below are common infinite sets and how they relate (in size).

## Naturals

$$
\mathbb{N} = \{0,1,2,3,\dots\}
$$

Countable: $|\mathbb{N}| = \aleph_0$.

## “Wholes” (often means nonnegative integers)

Many textbooks use “whole numbers” to mean $\{0,1,2,\dots\}$, i.e. $\mathbb{N}$.

## Integers

$$
\mathbb{Z} = \{\dots,-2,-1,0,1,2,\dots\}
$$

Countable: $|\mathbb{Z}| = \aleph_0$.

## Rationals

$$
\mathbb{Q} = \left\{\frac{p}{q} : p \in \mathbb{Z},\ q \in \mathbb{Z}\setminus\{0\}\right\}
$$

Countable: $|\mathbb{Q}| = \aleph_0$.

## Irrationals

Irrational numbers are:

$$
\mathbb{R}\setminus\mathbb{Q}.
$$

Uncountable.

## Reals

$$
\mathbb{R}
$$

Uncountable: $|\mathbb{R}| = \mathfrak{c}$.

## Complex

$$
\mathbb{C} = \{a+bi : a,b \in \mathbb{R},\ i^2=-1\}.
$$

Same cardinality as the reals:

$$
|\mathbb{C}| = |\mathbb{R}|.
$$
